## Characters
- **Kristian Hadeland** — narrator, 20, Norwegian photography student in London (Deptford), 1985-86. Ambitious, self-absorbed. Opens book stating intent to take his own life, writing from a house by the sea belonging to "Emil."
- **Hans** — Dutch artist, ~30, tall/lanky, works from disused garage near Peckham. Builds robotic rats (Shannon's maze) and turtles (Walter's turtles). Obsessed with mirrors, Marlowe, occult, biology/technology interface. Designing set for Vivian's Faustus. Building model of London with historical figures' initials.
- **Vivian Moore** — theatre director, late 20s/early 30s, dark hair, glasses. Directing Marlowe's Doctor Faustus. Sexual relationship with Kristian. Quotes Paul Becker. Asks Kristian to photograph Marlowe's London for programme.
- **Daniella** — designer's assistant on Faustus production. Dark-haired, warm. Kristian attracted to her. Mentions Becker quote she heard from Vivian.
- **Lars (Dad)** — Kristian's father, farmer, emotionally reserved. Overheard calling Kristian "a narcissist, through and through" and "a black hole." Catalyst for Kristian's estrangement.
- **Mum** — Danish-born, lived in Norway 30+ years. Quick, birdlike, sentimental, domineering. Mediates family tensions.
- **Liv** — Kristian's sister, 28. Thin, restless, history of drug use, multiple university dropouts. Overdoses on Christmas Eve. Frank and cutting with Kristian.
- **Helene** — Kristian's eldest sister, 32. Calm, domestic, resembles father. Has daughter Ane and husband Rickard.
- **Ane** — Helene's daughter, 8. Buck teeth, glasses. Unsettling scene where she touches Kristian inappropriately.
- **Rickard** — Helene's husband, bricklayer, overweight, quietly hostile to Kristian. Waiting to inherit family farm.
- **Vidar** — Kristian's childhood friend, studies CS at NTNU. Guileless, easily manipulated by Kristian as a boy. Identifies Hans's creations as classic computer science artifacts.
- **Rafael Figueroa** — guest lecturer, well-known Magnum war photographer. Devastating critique of Kristian's Vertigo series drives him to skip school.
- **George Hindley** — Kristian's regular photography tutor, 50s, gentle and encouraging.
- **Dark-haired man** — thin, possibly Egyptian, brown suit. Stares at Kristian in Soho cafe and again on Tube. Identity unknown. Creates paranoia.
- **Emil** — owns the house Kristian writes from. Mentioned only in opening.
- **Yelena** — addressed in opening, told "you know what's coming."
- **Stina** — nurse, 25, from Kristian's hometown. Brief encounter at Christmas, Kristian rejects her cruelly.
- **Collin** — tenant in Kristian's London building, from West Indies.
- **Liz** — student tenant downstairs, studies English lit, has boyfriend. Neurotic, reserved.
- **Anne Nymo** — Norwegian embassy official who comes to check on Kristian after parents report him missing.
- **Paul Becker** — historical figure (Austrian poet/occultist, 1883-1914). His quote "He who is dead is dead, and cannot be saved" appears on back of Daguerre photo sent anonymously to Kristian.

## Key Events
- **Opening frame**: Kristian writing from a coastal house, planning suicide. Addresses Emil (house owner) and Yelena.
- **1985 London**: Kristian arrives in Deptford to study photography. Meets Hans at a pub.
- **Hans's studio visit**: Kristian shown Shannon's maze (robotic rat) and Walter's turtles. Hans critiques Kristian's photos as "expression of an idea" lacking physical connection to the world.
- **Christmas in Norway**: Family gathering. Liv arrives late, overdoses on Christmas Eve. Ambulance called. Dad overheard calling Kristian a narcissist. Kristian photographs the empty house, flees back to London at dawn without telling anyone.
- **Ane incident**: 8-year-old niece touches Kristian inappropriately; he can't tell anyone.
- **Embassy visit**: Anne Nymo from Norwegian embassy finds Kristian after parents report him missing.
- **Hans and Marlowe**: Hans tells Kristian about Marlowe's death in Deptford, theory that Marlowe faked death and wrote Shakespeare's plays. Also talks about Daguerre's first photograph and claims the figure is the Devil.
- **Dead cat**: Kristian steals a dead cat from a vet's bin, boils it to photograph the skeleton (the "Frameworks" project).
- **Figueroa's critique**: Guest lecturer devastates Kristian's Vertigo series. Kristian stops attending school.
- **Vivian**: Sexual relationship begins. She directs Doctor Faustus, asks Kristian to photograph Marlowe's London.
- **The incident**: Kristian confronts a homeless man who stole his lighter. The man hits his head and is knocked unconscious. Kristian leaves. Later sees police at the scene — the man may be dead. A newspaper photo shows a blurry figure resembling Kristian. Hans notices the resemblance.
- **Anonymous Daguerre photo**: Someone sends Kristian a copy of Daguerre's Boulevard du Temple with the Becker quote on the back. Hans denies sending it. Vivian is suspected.
- **Daniella**: Kristian meets her through Faustus production. She mentions the Becker quote. Kristian is attracted to her.

## Themes & Questions
- Narcissism: Dad's diagnosis of Kristian, the Narcissus/Echo myth Hans tells, mirrors as closed loops
- Art and intention: Hans's critique that Kristian's photos show the photographer's intention rather than the world
- Marlowe/Shakespeare: doubling, faked death, assumed identity — parallels to Kristian's own situation?
- The occult thread: Hans's obsession with the Devil in Daguerre's photo, Paul Becker as occultist, Doctor Faustus, Cornelius Agrippa, Giordano Bruno
- Death and violence: Liv's overdose, the homeless man incident, Kristian's opening statement of suicidal intent
- The anonymous message: "He who is dead is dead, and cannot be saved. Choose life, Kristian, and you shall do well." — who sent it?
